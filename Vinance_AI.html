Project Statement


The goal of this project is to create an all-in-one financial app that can help young adults safely manage their finances while building wealth through AI insights. The app should offer a budgeting template, a financial advisor chatbot and a stock predicting AI.

Research Notes

My initial research focused on seeing what similar products alreaday existed and what I could learn from them. Since AI is stil relatively new to the world of finance, its potential has not been utilized. There are some recentrly created preducst that are attemtping to use it, which I've listed below. After researching similar products, I moved onto AI research to learn more about the best ways to use and integrate AI in this environment. This approach allowed me to identify the actions to do and avoid while integrating AI in financial sense.


Similar Products

  • Intellectia AI

    Intellectia is a recent startup that uses AI to sift through data to analyze and make predictions. Intellectia also offers a chatbot to get instant financial advice. The data analysis is also used to see trends, uncover price drivers and understand fluctuations. While this product offers a lot of financial aid, I believe this is too proffesional, my idea builds on it by making the service more accesible and user friendly.

  • Albert

    Albert is a great website that is using AI's potential to help people. Albert has done some things very right in their build. I hope for part of idea to compete with Albert.

  • Stanford project domcumentation

    This documentation says a lot about what to do and what not to do. It exlpores a lot of the programs limiations and optimazations that will help me make a better project.

  • Takeaways:

    • Process data efficiently by removing URLs, handling hashtags and adaptong for fiancial vocabulary
    • Incorporate a way to weight information based on liekes or shares
    • Use an optimized history of ~14 days
    • Recategorize information based on thier effects/insights eg: bullish, bearish, neutral
    • Use more than just sentiment becuase sentiment alon is not enough
    • Use large, diverse training datasets to offset chances of misleading predictions
    • Fine tune for timing of predcitions
